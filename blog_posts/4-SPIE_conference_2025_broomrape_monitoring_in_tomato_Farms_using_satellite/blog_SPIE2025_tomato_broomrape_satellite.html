Early Detection of Broomrape in Tomato Farms Using Satellite Imagery and Time-Series Analysis

Conference: SPIE Defense + Commercial Sensing 2025
Session: Autonomous Air and Ground Sensing Systems for Agricultural Optimization and Phenotyping X

Paper 13475-30 | Date & Time: 15 April 2025, 5:30 PM - 7:00 PM EDT | Sun Room 4, Ballroom Level

Authors: Mohammadreza Narimani (Presenter), Alireza Pourreza, Mohsen B. Mesgaran, Ali Moghimi, Hamid Jafarbiglu

Abstract

Early detection of broomrape in tomato fields is crucial to prevent significant yield losses. This study demonstrates a remote sensing approach using time-series satellite imagery and radiative transfer modeling to identify broomrape-infested areas early in the growing season. By analyzing spectral patterns between 600 and 1,500 Growing Degree Days (GDD), we distinguished infested from non-infested zones. Integrating PROSAIL modeling further characterized the biophysical and biochemical differences resulting from infestations. This scalable, cost-effective method offers timely intervention opportunities and supports improved management strategies for sustainable tomato production.
